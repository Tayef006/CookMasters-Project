Vegetarian Diet

What is the Vegetarian Diet?

A vegetarian diet is one that does not include any meat or seafood. However, there are many variants within the diet as some vegetarians choose to eat dairy products and eggs too while others abstain from it.


Vegetarian diets can have many health benefits. They can offer all the essential vitamins and minerals necessary for a long and healthy life provided they are well-planned.


One of the different types of vegetarian diets include: lacto-ovo-vegetarian - which consists of people who do not eat any meat or seafood, but include dairy foods such as eggs, milk and plant foods.

Benefits

  • Lower risk of chronic illnesses such as heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension.
  • Environmentally sustainable compared to meat-based diets.
  • Rich in vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, and fiber.
  • Potential weight management and improved overall health.

Disadvantages

  • Risk of deficiencies in protein, iron, or vitamin B12 without careful planning.
  • May require more effort to ensure balanced meals.
  • Limited options in some social or dining situations.

Popular Foods

Fruits:

Apples, bananas, berries, oranges, and more.

Vegetables:

Spinach, broccoli, carrots, zucchini, and others.

Legumes:

Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans.

Nuts & Seeds:

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds.

Grains:

Quinoa, brown rice, whole wheat bread, oats.

Dairy & Eggs:

Milk, yogurt, cheese, eggs for protein-rich options.
