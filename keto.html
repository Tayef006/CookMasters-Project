Keto Diet

What is a Keto Diet?

A ketogenic diet (or keto diet) is a high-fat, low-carbohydrate, and moderate protein eating plan which has the purpose of shifting the body's metabolism into a state called ketosis. In ketosis, the body will use fats instead of carbohydrates for energy which consequently leads to the production of molecules called ketones. This is due to the carbohydrate intake being drastically reduced.

Benefits

  • Effective weight loss.
  • Improved blood sugar and insulin levels.
  • Enhanced mental clarity and focus.
  • Higher energy levels.
  • Appetite control.
  • Reduced inflamation.
  • Water weight reduction.

Disadvantages

  • Nutritional deficiencies.
  • 'Keto Flu' - flu like symptoms at the first stage of the diet.
  • Constipation / Diarrhoea.
  • Bad breath.
  • Hair loss.
  • Reduced performance in high-intensity exercises.

Popular Foods

Healthy Fats:

Olive oil, avocados, butter, ghee, lard, tallow.

Protein Sources:

Beef, lamb, fatty fish, eggs, tofu.

Low-Carb Vegetables:

Spinach, brocolli, bell peppers, lettuce, mushrooms.

Keto-Friendly fruits:

Strawberries, raspberries, blackberries, olives, coconuts.

Alternative Grains and Flours:

Almond flour, coconut flour, psyllium husk, ground chia seeds

Dairy:

Cheese, heavy cream, full-fat yoghurt, coconut milk.
