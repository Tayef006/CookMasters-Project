Vegan Diet

What is the Vegan Diet?

A vegan diet is a way of eating that excludes all animal-derived products and focuses entirely on plant-based foods. This means avoiding not only meat but also other animal products such as dairy, eggs, honey, and any other ingredients sourced from animals. The vegan diet is rooted in the principle of reducing harm to animals, though people may adopt it for various reasons, including health benefits, environmental concerns, or ethical beliefs.

Benefits

  • Reduces the risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, diabetes and certain cancers.
  • Smaller carbon footprint compared to diets that include animal products
  • Vegans tend to have a lower and healthy BMI (Body Mass Index)
  • Improved digestion
  • Opposes exploitation of animals and cruelty towards them.

Disadvantages

  • Possible vitamin B12 deficiency
  • Possible iron deficiency
  • Lower than average protein intake
  • Missing out on beneficial omega-3 fatty acids from fish
  • Limited food options
  • Less convenience

Popular Foods

Fruits:

Apples, bananas, berries, oranges, and more.

Vegetables:

Spinach, broccoli, carrots, zucchini, and others.

Legumes:

Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans.

Nuts & Seeds:

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds.

Grains:

Quinoa, brown rice, whole wheat bread, oats.
