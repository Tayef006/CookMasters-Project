Meet Our Chefs


John Torode


John Torode is a renowned Australian-British chef, restaurateur, and television presenter, best known for his role as a judge on the popular cooking competition show MasterChef. Born in Melbourne, Australia, he began his culinary career at an early age, honing his skills under some of Australia's top chefs before moving to the UK in the 1990s. Torode quickly made a name for himself in London's vibrant restaurant scene, eventually becoming the head chef at the celebrated Mezzo restaurant and later launching his own ventures, including the acclaimed Smiths of Smithfield.


In addition to his success in the kitchen, John Torode is a household name thanks to his charismatic presence on television. Alongside Gregg Wallace, he has hosted and judged MasterChef and Celebrity MasterChef captivating audiences with his expertise, honest critiques, and passion for food. Torode's television career also includes food travel documentaries, where he explores diverse cuisines, blending his love of adventure with culinary discovery. His shows not only entertain but also inspire home cooks to experiment with new ingredients and techniques.


John Torode's influence extends beyonds the screen with a number of successful cookbooks to his name. His books, such as John Torode's Sydney to Seoul and My Kind of Food, reflects his love for global flavours and approachable cooking. A proponent of fresh, seasonal ingredients, he encourages people to find joy in cooking and sharing meals. With his rich culinary background and engaging personality, Torode continues to inspire a new generation of food enthusiasts, making him an invaluable figure in the world of gastronomy.

Nadiya Hussain


Nadiya Hussain is a British culinary icon whose journey from a home baker to a national treasure has captured hearts worldwide. She first rose to fame as the winner of The Great British Bake Off in 2015, where her creative flair, impeccable technique, and warm personality made her a standout contestant. Nadiya’s unforgettable final bake—a stunning Union Jack-inspired cake—symbolized her unique ability to merge tradition with innovation. But her story goes far beyond the kitchen; as a proud British Bangladeshi, Nadiya has used her platform to celebrate multiculturalism, breaking barriers and redefining what it means to be a modern chef in the UK.


What sets Nadiya apart is her ability to connect with audiences through her authentic storytelling and boundless creativity. Following her Bake Off win, she became a television favorite with shows like Nadiya's British Food Adventure and Nadiya Bakes. Her on-screen presence is refreshingly genuine, as she mixes humor with heartfelt reflections on family, culture, and her love of food. She’s also ventured into unexpected territory, writing children’s books, a memoir (Finding My Voice), and even crafting a best-selling line of cookbooks that are as approachable as they are inventive. From mouthwatering recipes for samosa pies to quick weeknight dinners, Nadiya’s work inspires both novice and seasoned cooks to embrace joy in the kitchen.


Beyond her culinary achievements, Nadiya Hussain is a trailblazer and an advocate for mental health awareness, openly discussing her own struggles with anxiety and using her experiences to inspire others. Her impact transcends food; she has become a voice for inclusivity, resilience, and self-expression. Whether she's creating a feast for her family or addressing Parliament about the importance of diversity, Nadiya embodies the spirit of perseverance and positivity. Her journey from a small kitchen in Luton to becoming one of the UK’s most beloved figures is a testament to the power of passion, courage, and a good slice of cake.

Matt Tebbutt


Matt Tebbutt is a charismatic British chef and television presenter who has become a familiar face in homes across the UK, thanks to his infectious enthusiasm for food and culinary storytelling. Best known as the host of BBC’s Saturday Kitchen, Matt brings a unique mix of expertise, humor, and relatability to the show, making cooking feel accessible and exciting for viewers of all skill levels. His easygoing style, paired with an impressive culinary pedigree, has cemented his reputation as one of the UK’s most engaging food personalities.


Before becoming a household name, Matt honed his skills at some of the world’s most prestigious kitchens. After training at Leiths School of Food and Wine, he worked under renowned chefs like Marco Pierre White and Alastair Little, absorbing techniques that would later shape his own cooking philosophy. Matt's approach to food is refreshingly honest: he champions seasonal ingredients, straightforward recipes, and the idea that cooking should be fun rather than intimidating. His time as the head chef at The Foxhunter, his acclaimed restaurant in Monmouthshire, showcased his talent for creating bold, flavor-packed dishes that highlighted local Welsh produce.


Beyond his work on Saturday Kitchen, Matt Tebbutt has made his mark with numerous TV appearances and cookbooks, including Matt Tebbutt’s Weekend and Guilty Pleasures. His shows often delve into the science and history of food, offering viewers a fascinating glimpse into why we eat the way we do. Whether he's breaking down a complex recipe, exploring the origins of a beloved dish, or bantering with celebrity guests, Matt’s passion for food shines through in everything he does. With his infectious energy and deep love for gastronomy, Matt Tebbutt continues to inspire audiences to embrace the joy of cooking.

Lorraine Pascale


Lorraine Pascale is a multi-talented culinary star whose journey from international supermodel to acclaimed chef and TV personality is as inspiring as it is fascinating. Known for her warm, approachable style and passion for baking, Lorraine first gained fame with her BBC series Baking Made Easy, where she demystified complex recipes with her signature charm and clarity. Her path to culinary success is uniquely unconventional—after a successful modeling career that saw her gracing the covers of major magazines, Lorraine shifted gears to pursue her lifelong love of food, earning a diploma from Leiths School of Food and Wine and training as a patisserie chef at prestigious establishments like The Wolseley and Claridge’s.


What makes Lorraine stand out in the crowded world of food personalities is her innate ability to make baking feel achievable and fun for everyone. Her cookbooks, including Home Cooking Made Easy and A Lighter Way to Bake, are filled with creative, health-conscious recipes that don't compromise on flavor or indulgence. From innovative twists on classics like her famous "I Can't Believe You Made That!" cake to clever time-saving techniques, Lorraine has built a reputation as a kitchen companion for busy home cooks looking to elevate their everyday meals and treats. Her work reflects a perfect balance of sophistication and practicality, appealing to both novice bakers and seasoned gourmands.


Beyond her culinary accolades, Lorraine Pascale is a role model and advocate for personal growth, resilience, and wellness. A champion of mental health awareness, she has openly shared her own experiences with adoption and self-discovery, inspiring fans to embrace their authentic selves. Lorraine has also taken her expertise to international audiences, appearing on shows like the Food Network’s Holiday Baking Championship in the U.S. With her dynamic career, approachable recipes, and empowering message, Lorraine Pascale continues to inspire millions to find joy not just in food, but in life’s delicious possibilities.
