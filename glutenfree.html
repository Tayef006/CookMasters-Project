Gluten-Free Diet

What is a Gluten-Free Diet?

A gluten-free diet excludes all foods containing gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. This diet is primarily followed by people with certain medical conditions such as celiac disease, non-celiac gluten sensitivity, or simply a wheat allergy.

Benefits

  • Lessened risk of celiac disease.
  • Lessened risk of non-celiac gluten sensitivity.
  • Improved digestion.
  • Enhanced nutrient absorption.
  • Higher energy levels.

Disadvantages

  • Fibre deficiency.
  • Over-reliance on processed foods may lead to weight gain and other health issues.
  • Quite expensive.

Popular Foods

Fruits:

Apples, bananas, berries, oranges, and more.

Vegetables:

Spinach, broccoli, carrots, zucchini, and others.

Legumes:

Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans.

Nuts & Seeds:

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds.

Gluten-Free Grains and Starches

Rice, quinoa, millet, amaranth, buckwheat, sorghum.

Dairy & Eggs:

Milk, yogurt, cheese, eggs for protein-rich options.
