Pescatarian Diet

What is the Pescatarian diet?

The Pescatarian diet is a plant-based diet which incorporates seafood as the main source of animal protein whilst excluding other meats like beef, pork and poultry. It has a heavy emphasis on whole, nutrient-dense foods with lots of high quality protein, minerals, vitamins and etcetera.

Benefits

  • Rich in Omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Nutrient dense.
  • Weight management.
  • Lower environmental impact.
  • Animal welfare is cared for.

Disadvantages

  • Risk of mercury exposure.
  • Vitamin B12 deficiencies.
  • Iron and Zinc deficiencies.
  • Expensive lifestyle.
  • Overfishing which harms marine ecosystems.

Popular Foods

Seafood:

Salmon, mackerel, tuna, cod, shrimp, crab, prawns

Vegetables:

Spinach, broccoli, carrots, radishes, tomatoes

Legumes:

Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans.

Nuts & Seeds:

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds.

Grains:

Quinoa, brown rice, whole wheat bread, oats.

Fruits

Pineapple, mango, kiwi, oranges, lemons, blueberries, pears
